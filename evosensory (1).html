The Five Modules
🎮 Classic
Core timing engine. Millisecond tap tracking with spatial audio, XAC haptic pillow, and adaptive hit window.
👁️ Oculomotor
Pursuits, saccades, depth pulse, and gaze hold — all synced to the beat. Optional camera eye tracking on Chrome.
🧠 Cognitive
Vocab spelling + syllables and math sequences spoken on the beat. Language and number fluency meets timing.
📷 Motion
Camera detects clapping, knee taps, marching, and jumps timed to the beat. Requires Chrome on a computer.
🎯 Active Games
Catch stars, pop balloons, kick soccer balls, duck and jump, balance zones. Body movement controls the game. Targets vestibular, bilateral coordination, motor planning, and dynamic balance. Requires Chrome on a computer.
How to Run a Session
1
Create a patient profile — tap + New Player on the next screen. Set name, age category, and training modality (hands or feet, bilateral or one side). Starting BPM is set automatically.
2
Pick a module and level — start with Classic → Level 1. Adjust BPM with the + / − buttons if needed. Other modules unlock as you explore.
3
Confirm hardware — a quick screen before each session asks what's connected (headphones, vibration, XAC pillow). Only what you confirm gets documented in the SOAP note.
4
Tap with the beat — tap the large button, the canvas, or press spacebar. One tap per beat. The app scores every response.
5
Get your SOAP note — when the session ends, tap Generate SOAP Note. Copy to clipboard or print. Edit before pasting into your EMR.

Lost? The full manual is at evolve-ot.com/resources — a printable 5-page PDF covering every feature, the interpretation scale, and SOAP note guide.
🎧 Use wired headphones. Bluetooth adds 100–200ms delay that completely defeats the therapeutic feedback loop. The stereo left/right error tones require precise timing to work.
📱 iPhone users: For full haptic feedback, tap Share → Add to Home Screen in Safari after this screen. Installed apps get better vibration access. The Android app (coming soon) has full haptics.
Hardware Setup
1
Wired headphones: Left ear = you're rushing. Right ear = you're dragging. This spatial feedback is the core mechanism.
2
Hand switches: Left → f, ←, z, Shift-L. Right → j, →, /, Ctrl-R. Any USB switch or jelly bean that sends a keystroke works instantly.
3
Foot switches: Same key mapping. Level 6 is designed specifically for bilateral foot tap training.
4
Xbox Adaptive Controller ($100): This is where EvoSensory transforms. Your existing AAC switch plugs directly into the XAC 3.5mm back jack — no adapter needed. Button 0 = LEFT, Button 1 = RIGHT. The XAC routes independent vibration to each side: left fires when you tap early, right fires when you tap late. Connect a haptic cushion to the output jack and the child feels timing feedback through their body.
5
No hardware? Tap the on-screen buttons, tap the canvas, or press any keyboard key. All modes work without accessories. Wired earbuds ($10) are the single best upgrade.
