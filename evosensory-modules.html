Classic
Core timing engine · Six progressive levels · Start every patient here

Classic Module

Classic is the foundation of EvoSensory and the only module with direct grounding in the rhythmic timing training evidence base. A patient taps in time with a metronome beat. The app measures accuracy, timing deviation, and session trend in real time. Spatial audio and haptic feedback close the loop immediately — the patient hears and feels whether each tap was early or late before the next beat arrives.

Why Classic is the evidence-based core

The core mechanism — millisecond-precise synchronization of voluntary movement to an external auditory beat, with immediate multimodal error feedback — is exactly what three decades of peer-reviewed research has measured and validated. The specific combination of timed tapping, spatial audio error feedback, bilateral alternation, and progressive tempo increase maps directly onto established protocols for improving cerebellar-basal ganglia timing circuits.

The other four modules are evidence-informed extensions — they apply timing training principles to additional domains (visual, cognitive, whole-body motor) that have their own research support, but the specific combined approach has not been independently validated as a clinical protocol.

Visual Modes

Falling Notes
Notes fall from the top. Patient taps when they reach the target zone. Good default for most patients.
Bubble Blast
Bubbles expand on the beat. Fun, motivating format — good for younger children or low-engagement sessions.
Target Range
Visual target at the bottom. Clean, minimal, less visually stimulating. Good for children who are over-responsive to busy visuals.
💰 Money Catcher
Stars fall from the sky — catch them with your hands. Perfect timing = $100 bill, good = $20, close = $5. Strong reward motivator. Wallet total accumulates through the session.
💰 Money Catcher
Stars fall from the sky — catch them with your hands. Perfect timing = $100 bill. Strong motivator for reward-driven patients.
Blind Training Mode

Available in Settings. The visual display fades after 10 seconds, leaving only audio and haptic feedback. Trains pure internal timing without visual anchoring. Use when a patient has become reliant on the visual cue and you want to build internalization.

Best For

Every patient — ADHD, DCD, dyspraxia, TBI, stroke, autism, dyslexia, Parkinson's, older adults with balance concerns. Classic is the most clinically versatile module and should be the starting point for everyone.

Oculomotor Module

Evidence-informed extension

The Oculomotor module applies rhythmic timing training principles to visual-motor integration. Smooth pursuits, saccades, and fixation training each have independent research support in vision therapy and neurorehabilitation. The specific combination with a timed tapping task is EvoSensory's design — it has not been independently validated as a standalone clinical protocol. Use Classic as the primary intervention and Oculomotor as a complement when visual-motor timing is a treatment goal.

Visual targets move in sync with the metronome beat. The patient taps when the target reaches a cued position. The app measures how accurately the tap is timed to the visual event — not whether the patient's eyes tracked correctly. Tapping is used as a motor proxy for visual tracking.

An optional camera-based gaze estimation toggle (Phase 2) overlays a real-time gaze crosshair on the canvas during pursuits and saccades and records average gaze lag. This is screening-level data — clearly labeled in the SOAP note. It does not replace a behavioral optometry evaluation.

Sub-Modes

Smooth Pursuits
Target sweeps left and right continuously. Patient taps at each turnaround. Targets smooth visual tracking and bilateral timing.
Saccadic Jumps
Target jumps between two boxes on each beat. Patient taps at each appearance. Targets saccadic initiation speed and timing accuracy.
Depth Pulse
A circle expands from tiny to full screen on each beat. Patient taps at maximum expansion. Targets visual convergence and accommodation training.
Gaze Hold
Stationary target — patient holds gaze on it while tapping the beat. Targets sustained visual attention and dual-task timing.

Best For

Patients with visual processing difficulties, reading challenges, post-concussion visual symptoms, convergence insufficiency, or any condition where visual-motor timing integration is a treatment target. Refer to behavioral optometry when gaze screening data suggests significant tracking deficits.

Cognitive Module

Evidence-informed extension

The Cognitive module applies rhythmic timing to language and numeracy. Phonological timing research (particularly for dyslexia) supports the use of beat-based auditory processing — Tierney & Kraus (2014, PMID: 25538596) established links between auditory-motor entrainment and phonological skill. The specific letter-by-syllable + tapping format is EvoSensory's design. Use Classic as the primary protocol and Cognitive as a complement when phonological processing or math fluency are treatment goals. Collaborate with SLP for language targets.

In Cognitive mode, speech replaces the beat sound. The app speaks on the beat — the patient taps along while processing the spoken content. This creates a dual-task that directly targets phonological processing and number fluency alongside rhythmic timing.

Sub-Modes

Vocabulary Mode
Words are spelled letter by letter, broken into syllables, and spoken whole — all timed to the beat. BUT-TER-FLY. Targets phonological awareness and auditory processing.
Math Mode
Counting sets, addition, and multiplication spoken on the beat. "3 plus 4 equals." A dot counter visual supports numbers up to 24. Targets number fluency and working memory.

Best For

Dyslexia, reading delays, phonological processing difficulties, math fluency challenges, ADHD with academic impact. Strong dual-task training for any patient who needs to practice simultaneous cognitive and motor demands. Use in collaboration with SLP when targeting phonological goals.

Motion Module

Evidence-informed extension

The Motion module applies rhythmic timing to whole-body movement. Rhythmic Auditory Stimulation (RAS) for gait has strong evidence in Parkinson's and stroke (Thaut et al., 2015, PMID: 26407995). The specific MediaPipe-based gesture detection and scoring approach is EvoSensory's design and has not been independently validated. Use Classic as the primary timing protocol and Motion when whole-body coordination or gait are treatment targets.

The camera detects specific body movements and scores them against the metronome beat. No buttons needed — the gesture is the tap. A live skeleton overlay shows the detected landmarks on screen during the session.

The Start button activates automatically when the camera detects the required body landmarks in frame. For full-body modes, it waits until both ankles are visible.

Requires Chrome on Windows, Mac, or Android. Falls back to basic frame-differencing motion detection if MediaPipe fails to load.

Sub-Modes

👏 Clapping
Both wrists come together on the beat. Waist-up camera. Targets bilateral upper extremity coordination and rhythmic motor timing.
🤸 Knee Taps / Midline
Right hand to left knee, left hand to right knee, alternating. Targets midline crossing, bilateral integration, and ideomotor praxis.
🦵 Marching
Alternating knee raises above hip height. Full body required. Targets bilateral lower extremity coordination, postural control, and rhythmic sequencing.
⬆️ Jumps
Both feet leave the ground on the beat. Full body + jump clearance required. Targets bilateral lower extremity coordination, vestibular processing, and motor planning.

Best For

Any patient where whole-body movement is a therapy target — DCD, bilateral coordination deficits, gait training, vestibular processing, postural control, motor planning. Strong for telehealth observation of movement quality.

Active Games Module

Evidence-informed extension

Active Games applies body-movement control to game-based therapy. Game-based interventions for motor learning and balance have general research support, but the specific body-tracking game format here is EvoSensory's own design. It is not a validated clinical protocol. Use as a complement to Classic-based timing training — particularly for motivation, engagement, and functional movement practice — not as a replacement for a structured timing intervention.

Body position and movement control five different games. The camera tracks the patient's body using MediaPipe Pose and maps their position to in-game actions. No tapping required — movement is the input.

Requires Chrome on Windows, Mac, or Android.

The Five Games

⭐ Catch the Stars
Stars fall from the top. Side-step left or right to align under them. Targets lateral weight shifting, vestibular processing, and dynamic balance.
🎈 Pop Balloons
Balloons float up. Reach overhead with the matching hand to pop them. Targets shoulder ROM, overhead reach, and visual-motor integration.
⚽ Kick Soccer
Balls roll in from either side. Kick with the matching foot. Targets unilateral lower extremity motor control and single-leg balance.
🏃 Duck & Jump
Obstacles slide in from the right. Duck under high obstacles, jump over low ones. Targets motor planning, reaction time, and dynamic whole-body movement.
↔️ Side Steps & Balance
Highlighted zones appear on left or right. Step into the zone and hold still — the longer you hold, the higher the score multiplier. Targets dynamic and static balance and proprioception.
Clinical targets across Active Games

Vestibular processing · Dynamic balance · Static balance · Postural control · Proprioception · Upper extremity reach and ROM · Unilateral lower extremity control · Lateral weight shifting · Motor planning · Reaction time · Visual-motor integration

Best For

Patients with vestibular processing challenges, balance deficits, postural instability, fall risk, lower extremity coordination goals, or any patient who needs a movement-based engagement format. Strong for older adults, DCD, TBI, stroke rehab, and patients who need a break from seated tap-based training.
