What It Is

What Is EvoSensory?

EvoSensory is a rhythmic timing training platform built by a pediatric OT. A patient taps in time with a metronome beat. The app measures — to the millisecond — how accurately they hit the beat, whether they tend to tap early or late, and whether their timing gets better or worse as the session progresses. That data becomes a SOAP note draft automatically.

It runs in any browser. Nothing to install. No subscription required.

The Evidence

Why Rhythmic Timing Training Works

Rhythmic timing training has a strong evidence base built over three decades of research across OT, PT, special education, and neuroscience. The core mechanism is well established: the brain's internal timing system — involving the cerebellum, basal ganglia, supplementary motor area, and prefrontal cortex — can be trained through structured, repetitive practice synchronizing movement to an external beat.

When this system is less efficient, the effects are visible in daily life: impulsivity, bilateral coordination difficulty, slow processing, reading challenges, handwriting struggles, and poor motor sequencing. EvoSensory makes timing errors measurable and documentable in a way a therapist watching cannot achieve with the naked eye.

ADHD & Attention
Children with ADHD show measurable timing deficits independent of inattention. Rhythmic training reduces impulsivity and improves sustained attention through cerebellar-prefrontal pathways.
DCD & Bilateral Coordination
Rhythmic timing training improves alternating limb timing and interlimb synchronization in DCD. Improvements transfer to functional bilateral tasks.
Reading & Phonological Processing
Temporal processing underlies phoneme discrimination. Rhythmic training improves reading fluency in children with dyslexia through auditory timing mechanisms.
Parkinson's & Stroke
Rhythmic auditory stimulation has some of the strongest evidence in Parkinson's research for gait. Rhythmic cuing improves bilateral motor retraining post-stroke.

The Platform

Five Training Modules

🎮
Classic Active
Core timing engine. Six progressive levels. Spatial audio, haptic feedback, XAC integration. Start here with every patient.
👁️
Oculomotor Active
Pursuits, saccades, depth pulse, gaze hold — all synced to the beat. Optional camera eye tracking on Chrome desktop.
🧠
Cognitive Active
Vocabulary and math spoken on the beat. Phonological processing and number fluency meet rhythmic timing.
📷
Motion Beta
Camera detects clapping, knee taps, marching, and jumps timed to the beat. Chrome on a computer required.
🎯
Active Games Beta
Five camera games controlled by body movement — catch stars, pop balloons, kick soccer, duck and jump, balance zones. Targets vestibular, bilateral coordination, motor planning, and dynamic balance. Chrome on a computer required.

No Technology Required

Want to Start Without the App?

EvoSensory exists because not every session has device access. The clinical rationale for rhythmic timing training doesn't change when the app isn't available. A structured low-tech protocol using a free metronome app and everyday materials can be used as a home program, a school-based handout, or a starting point before introducing EvoSensory in clinic.
