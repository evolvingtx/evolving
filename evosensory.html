The Four Modules
🎮 Classic
Core timing engine. Millisecond tap tracking with XAC haptic pillow feedback and spatial audio.
👁️ Oculomotor
Smooth pursuits and saccadic jump training synced to the metronome beat.

📷 Motion
Webcam movement tracking — clapping, knee taps, midline crossing. Coming soon.
🎧 Use wired headphones. Bluetooth adds 100–200ms delay that completely defeats the therapeutic feedback loop. The stereo left/right error tones require precise timing to work.
📱 iPhone users: For full haptic feedback, tap Share → Add to Home Screen in Safari after this screen. Installed apps get better vibration access. The Android app (coming soon) has full haptics.
Hardware Setup
1
Wired headphones: Left ear = you're rushing. Right ear = you're dragging. This spatial feedback is the core mechanism.
2
Hand switches: Left → f, ←, z, Shift-L. Right → j, →, /, Ctrl-R. Any USB switch or jelly bean that sends a keystroke works instantly.
3
Foot switches: Same key mapping. Level 6 is designed specifically for bilateral foot tap training.
4
Xbox Adaptive Controller ($100): This is where EvoSensory transforms. Your existing AAC switch plugs directly into the XAC 3.5mm back jack — no adapter needed. Button 0 = LEFT, Button 1 = RIGHT. The XAC routes independent vibration to each side: left fires when you tap early, right fires when you tap late. Connect a haptic cushion to the output jack and the child feels timing feedback through their body.
5
No hardware? Tap the on-screen buttons, tap the canvas, or press any keyboard key. All modes work without accessories. Wired earbuds ($10) are the single best upgrade.
